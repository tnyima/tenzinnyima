Tibet

Tibet was an independent country in Asia. The main language spoken in Tibet is Tibetan, however, there are also other minor language that is spoken there as well. Tibet consist of over 6 million Tibetans. However, in 1959, Tibet officially lost its Indepence after the forceful occupation of China led by the Communist Party. Due to this event, there are over 100,000 Tibetans residing all over the world, with the majority in India. The current Tibetan government resides in North India as an exile government.
